<p><p>The explosion relief doors market comprises two main types: bottom hinged and top hinged explosion relief doors. Bottom hinged doors are designed to open at the bottom, allowing the explosion force to be directed downwards, while top hinged doors open at the top, diverting the explosion force upwards. Both types of doors serve the purpose of preventing excessive pressure buildup during explosions and minimizing damage to nearby structures or equipment. These doors are crucial safety mechanisms widely used in industries handling volatile substances and materials.</p></p>
