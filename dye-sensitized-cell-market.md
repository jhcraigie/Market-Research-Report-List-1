<p><p>Dye-sensitized cells (DSC) are a type of solar cell that utilize different types of semiconductor materials as the working electrode. These materials include titanium dioxide (TiO2), tin dioxide (SnO2), zinc oxide (ZnO), niobium oxide (Nb2O), and others. TiO2 is the most commonly used material due to its stability and low cost. SnO2 offers higher electron mobility and increased efficiency. ZnO provides flexibility and is suitable for large-scale production. Nb2O exhibits enhanced light absorption properties. Other materials are being researched for their unique properties and potential improvements in DSC technology.</p></p>
