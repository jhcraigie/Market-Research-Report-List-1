<p><p>PET tracers are substances used in positron emission tomography (PET) imaging to track and visualize specific biological processes in the body. Radioactive oxygen and radiocarbon are two types of PET tracers commonly used. Radioactive oxygen, in the form of water labeled with an isotope of oxygen, is utilized to study cerebral blood flow and metabolism. Radiocarbon, also known as carbon-11, is used to investigate brain glucose metabolism. Other types of PET tracers include fluorine-18, nitrogen-13, and gallium-68, which are used for different applications such as tumor detection and evaluation of cardiac function.</p></p>
