<p><p>Respiratory tract infections (RTIs) are common infections that affect the nose, throat, and lungs. These infections are typically caused by viruses, bacteria, or fungi and can range from mild to severe. The treatment of RTIs depends on the underlying cause and severity of the infection.</p><p>For viral RTIs, symptomatic treatment is usually recommended, which may include rest, adequate fluid intake, and over-the-counter medications to relieve symptoms such as fever, cough, and congestion. Antibiotics are not effective against viral infections. However, bacterial RTIs such as pneumonia or bacterial sinusitis may require antibiotic treatment to clear the infection.</p><p>In severe cases or when complications arise, hospitalization and intravenous antibiotics may be necessary.
