<p><p>In-Vitro Fertilization (IVF) is a reproductive technology that involves the fertilization of an egg with sperm outside the body in a laboratory setting. It is commonly used for couples who are struggling with infertility or have certain genetic disorders. The process typically involves stimulating the ovaries to produce multiple eggs, retrieving the eggs through a minor surgical procedure, fertilizing the eggs with sperm in a laboratory, and then transferring the resulting embryos into the woman's uterus.</p><p>The In-Vitro Fertilization (IVF) market has witnessed significant growth over the years and is expected to continue to grow at a CAGR of 12.3% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors such as increasing infertility rates, advancements in reproductive technologies, growing awareness about IVF treatments, and the rising trend of delayed pregnancies. Additionally, the increasing number of same-sex couples and single parents opting for IVF has also contributed to the market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, the market has witnessed several trends in recent years. One of the key trends is the increasing use of preimplantation genetic testing (PGT) in IVF procedures. PGT allows for the screening of embryos for genetic disorders before they are transferred to the uterus, thereby increasing the chances of a successful pregnancy. Another trend is the growing popularity of frozen embryo transfers (FET) compared to fresh embryo transfers. FET has shown higher success rates and is less invasive, leading to its increasing adoption.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements such as time-lapse imaging, improved cryopreservation techniques, and the use of automated systems in IVF laboratories have also contributed to the market growth. These advancements have resulted in better outcomes, increased efficiency, and improved patient experience.</p><p>Overall, the In-Vitro Fertilization (IVF) market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years, driven by factors such as increasing infertility rates, advancements in reproductive technologies, and the rising trend of delayed pregnancies. The adoption of trends like PGT and FET, along with technological advancements, will further contribute to the market growth.</p></p>
