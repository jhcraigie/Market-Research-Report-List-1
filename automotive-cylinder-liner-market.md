<p><p>The automotive cylinder liner market comprises two major types: casting iron and aluminum alloys. Casting iron liners are commonly used due to their excellent thermal conductivity, high strength, and wear resistance. They are typically used in heavy-duty vehicles and engines that require high durability and heat dissipation. On the other hand, aluminum alloy liners are known for their lightweight nature, which helps in reducing the overall weight of the engine. These liners are mainly used in passenger cars and light commercial vehicles to enhance fuel efficiency and improve performance.</p></p>
