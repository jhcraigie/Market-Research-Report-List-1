<p><p>Commercial boilers are large-size boilers that are primarily used in commercial buildings such as hotels, hospitals, schools, and office complexes to provide heating and hot water. These boilers are designed to efficiently heat water or produce steam, which is then distributed through a network of pipes to various parts of the building. They can be fueled by natural gas, oil, or electricity.</p><p>The commercial boilers market is experiencing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing demand for heating and hot water in commercial buildings is driving the market growth. Additionally, the rising focus on energy efficiency and sustainability is prompting the adoption of high-efficiency commercial boilers that can help reduce energy consumption and carbon emissions.</p><p>Furthermore, stringent government regulations and policies aimed at reducing greenhouse gas emissions are also contributing to the market growth. For instance, the European Union has set targets to reduce carbon emissions, which is driving the adoption of energy-efficient commercial boilers in the region.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements and innovations in commercial boilers are also fueling the market growth. Manufacturers are developing advanced controls and monitoring systems for commercial boilers to enhance their performance and efficiency.</p><p>The market is also witnessing several trends. One prominent trend is the increasing use of condensing boilers, which are highly efficient and can recover heat from the flue gases. Another trend is the growing adoption of biomass boilers, which use renewable sources such as wood pellets or agricultural waste as fuel.</p><p>Overall, the commercial boilers market is projected to grow at a CAGR of 9.6% during the forecast period.
