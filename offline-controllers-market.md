<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Offline controllers are widely used in various electronic devices and systems. There are two main types of offline controllers: current mode and voltage mode. </p><p>In current mode controllers, the output current is directly regulated, allowing for fast response to load changes. They are suitable for applications with large variations in load current.</p><p>Voltage mode controllers regulate the output voltage by modulating the duty cycle of the PWM signal. They provide stable voltage regulation and are commonly used in systems with a relatively stable load. </p><p>Both types have their specific advantages and are chosen based on the requirements of the application at hand.</p></p>
