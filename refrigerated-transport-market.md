<p><p>Refrigerated transport refers to the transportation of perishable goods such as food or pharmaceuticals in temperature-controlled environments to maintain their freshness and quality. The market types for refrigerated transport include airways, roadways, and seaways. Airways involve using airplanes equipped with refrigeration systems for transporting goods quickly over long distances. Roadways involve using refrigerated trucks or vans to transport goods over shorter distances within a country or region. Seaways involve using refrigerated containers on ships for transporting goods internationally through sea routes. Each market type caters to different transportation needs based on distance and time constraints.</p></p>
