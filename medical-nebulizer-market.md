<p><p>A medical nebulizer is a device used to deliver medication directly to the lungs for the treatment of respiratory conditions such as asthma, COPD (chronic obstructive pulmonary disease), and cystic fibrosis. Nebulizers are often preferred by patients who have difficulty using inhalers.</p><p>The global medical nebulizer market is experiencing significant growth due to several factors. The increasing prevalence of respiratory diseases, such as asthma and COPD, along with the rising geriatric population, is driving the demand for medical nebulizers. <p><p>A medical nebulizer is a device that converts liquid medication into a fine mist for inhalation. There are two types of nebulizers: benchtop and portable. Benchtop nebulizers are larger, stationary devices that are commonly found in hospitals or clinics. They require an electrical outlet for power and are suitable for use at home. Portable nebulizers, on the other hand, are smaller and lightweight, designed to be easily carried and used on the go. They can be battery-operated or rechargeable, providing convenience to patients who need regular treatments outside their home environment.</p></p>
