<p><p>The hybrid fire alarm system market consists of various types such as conventional mode, addressable mode, standalone mode, and others. In the conventional mode, fire detectors are connected to specific zones with alarm panels indicating the area of fire. The addressable mode allows individual devices, such as detectors and manual call points, to have unique addresses for precise identification of the fire location. Standalone mode functions independently without any connection to a central control panel. Other modes include wireless and analog systems, offering different features and capabilities to cater to diverse fire safety requirements.</p></p>
