<p><p>The plastic optical fiber market is segmented into two types: PMMA (Polymer Polymethylmethacrylate) and Perfluorinated Polymers. PMMA is a commonly used plastic material due to its high transparency and low cost, making it suitable for short-range communication applications like local area networks. On the other hand, Perfluorinated Polymers have excellent light transmission properties, making them ideal for long-range communication systems like telecommunication networks. Choosing the right type of plastic optical fiber depends on the specific requirements of the communication system.</p></p>
