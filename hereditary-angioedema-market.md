Conventional treatments for HAE include on-demand therapy, prophylactic therapy, and acute treatment options. <p><p>Hereditary Angioedema (HAE) is a rare genetic disorder characterized by recurring episodes of severe swelling in various body parts. The market for HAE treatments includes three main types: C1 esterase inhibitor, kallikrein inhibitor, and selective bradykinin B2 receptor antagonist. C1 esterase inhibitor therapies aim to replace the deficient protein, preventing swelling attacks. Kallikrein inhibitors target the enzymes responsible for triggering swelling. Selective bradykinin B2 receptor antagonists block the receptors that exacerbate swelling. Each type offers different mechanisms to manage and prevent HAE symptoms, providing options for patients based on their specific needs.</p></p>
