<p><p>Safety relays are devices used to monitor and control safety-related functions in machinery and equipment. There are three main types of safety relays in the market: Electromechanical relays, Solid State relays, and Thermal relays.</p><p>Electromechanical relays use mechanical contacts to control the circuit. They are robust and suitable for harsh environments. Solid State relays use semiconductors like transistors to switch the circuit. They are more compact, reliable, and have a longer lifespan. Thermal relays protect against overheating by monitoring temperature and interrupting the circuit when necessary. They are commonly used in motor protection.</p></p>
