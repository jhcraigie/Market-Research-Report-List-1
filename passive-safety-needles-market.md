<p><p>Passive Safety Needles are medical devices designed to minimize the risk of needlestick injuries. There are two types of Passive Safety Needles: Automatic Injection and Manual Injection. </p><p>Automatic Injection needles feature mechanisms that automatically retract or shield the needle after use, reducing the chances of accidental needlestick injuries. These needles are convenient and user-friendly, as the safety feature is integrated into the device design.</p><p>Manual Injection needles, on the other hand, require manual activation of the safety feature after use. This type of needle provides healthcare professionals with more control over safety, but requires additional steps to ensure needle protection.</p></p>
