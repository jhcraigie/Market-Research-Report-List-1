<p><p>MRI contrast media are substances used to enhance the visibility of internal body structures during magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) scans. There are two types of contrast media available on the market: injection type and oral type. Injection type contrast media are administered through intravenous injection and rapidly distribute in the body, providing immediate enhancement. Oral type contrast media are ingested orally and gradually accumulate in the gastrointestinal tract, enhancing the visibility of the stomach and intestines during MRI scans. Both types play a crucial role in improving diagnostic accuracy and detecting abnormalities in MRI images.</p></p>
