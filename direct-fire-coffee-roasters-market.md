<p>&nbsp;</p>
<p><p>Direct-fire coffee roasters are categorized into two main types: solid drum roasters and perforated drum roasters. Solid drum roasters consist of a closed drum where the coffee beans are heated directly by a flame underneath. This type ensures uniform heat distribution and retains more flavors. Perforated drum roasters, on the other hand, have small holes in the drum to allow airflow. This type promotes even roasting by exposing the beans to both convective and conductive heat, resulting in a consistent and high-quality roast.</p></p>
