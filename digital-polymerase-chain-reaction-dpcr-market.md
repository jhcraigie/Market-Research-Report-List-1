<p><p>Digital Polymerase Chain Reaction (dPCR) is a cutting-edge technology used in molecular biology to detect and quantify specific DNA sequences. It amplifies tiny amounts of DNA into millions of copies for analysis. There are two types of dPCR markets: Droplet Digital PCR and Chip-based Digital PCR. In droplet dPCR, the sample is divided into thousands of individual droplets, each containing DNA molecules, which are then analyzed and counted. On the other hand, chip-based dPCR involves loading the DNA sample onto a microchip, where the amplification and analysis take place.</p></p>
