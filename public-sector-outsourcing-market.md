<p><p>Public sector outsourcing refers to the practice of contracting out government functions and services to external vendors or service providers. Firstly, governments worldwide are under pressure to reduce their budget deficits and public spending, which prompts them to explore outsourcing as a cost-saving measure. Additionally, advancements in technology have enabled outsourcing vendors to provide efficient and secure services to governments.</p><p>Moreover, outsourcing allows governments to access specialized skills and expertise, which may not be available in-house. This enables them to improve service delivery and efficiency in areas such as healthcare, transportation, education, and defense.
